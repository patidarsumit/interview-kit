# 01. Generic HR Questions

These answers work for most companies.

Do not memorize word-by-word. Practice the flow and speak naturally.

Useful speaking phrases:

```text
In my current role...
I usually focus on...
One example from my project is...
I have been working on...
I am looking for...
```

## Tell Me About Yourself

Strong answer:

```text
I am a Senior Software Engineer with around 6.5 years of experience, primarily working with Angular, TypeScript, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS.

In my current role, I have worked on enterprise web applications for hospitality and government clients. My work includes building reusable Angular components, complex reactive forms, API integrations, role-based access flows, performance optimization, accessibility improvements, and automated testing.

I have also worked on code reviews, mentoring junior developers, CI/CD practices, and production issue support.

Along with frontend, I have exposure to Node.js, Express.js, GraphQL basics, Docker, AWS basics, and modern development tools, which helps me understand end-to-end application flow.

I am now looking for a role where I can contribute strongly with my Angular/frontend expertise and also grow further in architecture, full-stack understanding, and enterprise delivery.
```

Short answer:

```text
I am a Senior Software Engineer with 6.5+ years of experience, mainly in Angular and TypeScript. I have worked on scalable enterprise applications, reusable components, performance optimization, accessibility, testing, and CI/CD. I also mentor juniors and collaborate with QA, backend, UX, and product teams. I am looking for a role where I can contribute technically and grow further in architecture and full-stack engineering.
```

```text
Before that, I worked on CWC, which involved employee management, approvals, training, feedback, and role-based access. I also worked on HRMS modules like payroll, attendance, and employee management.

Overall, my experience is mainly around building scalable frontend applications with strong focus on quality, performance, accessibility, and maintainability.
```

## Why Are You Looking For Change?

```text
I have learned a lot in my current organization and got strong experience in Angular, enterprise UI development, performance, accessibility, testing, and mentoring.

Now I am looking for a larger opportunity where I can work on more complex systems, take more ownership, and grow further in architecture, modern engineering practices, and full-stack understanding.
```

Avoid:

```text
My company is bad.
I only want salary.
I am bored.
My manager is not good.
```

## Why This Company?

```text

```

```text
I am interested in this company because it works on large-scale digital products and enterprise technology solutions. The role needs Angular, frontend architecture, performance, testing, and collaboration with multiple teams, which aligns well with my experience.
```

## What Are Your Strengths?

```text
One of my key strengths is ownership. I take responsibility for my work and try to deliver with quality, not just complete the task.

Another strength is problem-solving. I stay calm during issues, analyze the root cause, and then work toward a practical solution.
```

## What Are Your Weaknesses?

Safe answer:

```text
Earlier, I sometimes spent extra time refining a solution to make it more optimal.

I have improved by balancing quality with delivery timelines, discussing tradeoffs early, and focusing first on business value while still keeping the code maintainable.
```

Alternative:

```text
Sometimes I used to take too much ownership and try to solve everything myself. I have improved by communicating earlier, involving the right people, and delegating or asking for support when needed.
```

## How Do You Handle Pressure?

```text
When there is pressure, I first clarify priority and deadline. Then I break the work into must-have and nice-to-have items.

I communicate risks early, give regular progress updates, and avoid compromising critical quality areas like security, validation, or production stability.

If everything cannot be delivered safely in one release, I suggest phased delivery with clear tradeoffs.
```

## How Do You Handle Conflict With A Colleague?

```text
I try to keep conflict focused on the problem, not the person.

If there is a technical disagreement, I compare approaches based on maintainability, performance, timeline, testing, and team standards.

If needed, I involve a senior person or discuss it in code review. My goal is to choose the best solution for the project, not to prove my point.
```

## Have You Mentored Anyone?

```text

I usually explain not only what to change, but why a particular approach is better in terms of readability, maintainability, performance, or testing.

I have also helped define coding standards and reusable component patterns so the team can deliver more consistently.
```

## Additional Common HR Questions Companies Ask

I checked common HR/behavioral question patterns for experienced software engineers and these are worth preparing too.

## Walk Me Through Your Career Progression

```text
I started my career in 2019 as a software/frontend developer and gradually built strong experience in Angular and enterprise web applications.

In the initial phase, I worked on HRMS modules like payroll, attendance, employee management, authentication, and role-based access. That helped me build a strong foundation in business-critical forms and workflows.

After that, I worked on CWC, where I handled employee management, approval workflows, training modules, feedback systems, and RBAC across multiple user roles.

In my recent work on Stayntouch, I moved into more complex enterprise frontend responsibilities like booking flows, rate configuration, real-time pricing modules, reusable component design, performance improvement, accessibility, testing, and mentoring.

Recently, I have also been involved in IB-X, an AI/workflow platform with a drag-and-drop workflow builder.

So my career progression has been from feature development to owning complex UI modules, improving quality, mentoring juniors, and contributing to scalable frontend architecture.
```

## Do You Prefer Working Independently Or In A Team?

```text
I am comfortable with both.

For focused development or debugging, I can work independently and take ownership. But for requirement clarity, architecture decisions, API contracts, UX discussions, testing, and release planning, teamwork is very important.

In real projects, good delivery needs both individual ownership and strong collaboration.
```

## Do You Prefer Startup Or Established Company?

```text
I am open to both, but I evaluate the role based on learning, project quality, engineering culture, and long-term growth.

In an established company, I value structured processes, enterprise-scale systems, and mature delivery practices.

In a startup, I value ownership, speed, and broader responsibility.

At this stage of my career, I am looking for a role where I can contribute deeply as a senior frontend engineer and grow technically, regardless of company size.
```

## Describe A Time You Failed Or Made A Mistake

```text
In one situation, I underestimated the impact of a UI change because it looked small from the frontend side, but it affected validation behavior in multiple flows.

Once QA identified the issue, I analyzed the root cause, fixed it, and also added better test coverage around that validation flow.

The learning was that even small UI changes can have business impact in enterprise applications, so now I check related flows, edge cases, and regression impact more carefully.
```

## Tell Me About A Time You Received Critical Feedback

```text
Earlier in my career, I received feedback that my implementation was technically correct but could be easier for other developers to maintain.

I took that seriously and started focusing more on naming, smaller components, better separation of logic, and documentation where needed.

That feedback helped me improve from just writing working code to writing maintainable code.
```

## Tell Me About A Disagreement With Your Manager Or Senior

```text
If I disagree with a manager or senior, I try to discuss it with data and reasoning.

For example, if there are two implementation approaches, I compare them based on maintainability, delivery timeline, performance, testing, and future changes.

If the final decision is different from my suggestion, I still support the team decision professionally, unless there is a serious quality or security risk that needs escalation.
```

## How Do You Handle Ambiguous Requirements?

```text
When requirements are unclear, I first ask clarifying questions and try to understand the business goal.

Then I identify assumptions, edge cases, API dependencies, validation rules, and user flows.

If needed, I create a small UI flow, document assumptions, and confirm with product/UX/backend before implementation.

This avoids rework and helps the team align early.
```

## What Makes A Good Software Engineer?

```text
A good software engineer does not only write code. They understand the problem, think about maintainability, communicate clearly, test their work, consider edge cases, and take ownership.

A great engineer also improves team quality through code reviews, mentoring, documentation, reusable patterns, and better engineering practices.
```

```text
I try to give constructive comments with reasoning instead of just saying something is wrong.
```
