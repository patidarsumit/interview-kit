# 02. Salary Negotiation

Salary negotiation should be handled calmly and professionally.

Goal:

```text
Get a fair offer without sounding greedy, rigid, or desperate.
```

## Important Mindset

Do not think negotiation means fighting.

Negotiation means:

- understanding the role
- knowing your value
- discussing compensation professionally
- considering full package
- making a decision based on role + money + growth

## Your Value Points

Use these as your negotiation base:

- 6.5+ years experience
- Angular and TypeScript specialist
- enterprise application experience
- hospitality and government domain exposure
- performance improvement work
- WCAG accessibility experience
- 90%+ unit-test coverage claim
- Cypress/E2E exposure
- CI/CD exposure
- mentoring 4 juniors
- reusable component architecture
- Node/React/GraphQL/AWS basics

You are not negotiating only as a coder. You are negotiating as a senior frontend engineer who can own delivery quality.

## When HR Asks Expected CTC Early

Best answer:

```text
I would like to understand the role, responsibilities, project expectations, and overall compensation structure first.

Based on my 6.5+ years of Angular/frontend experience and current market standards, I am looking for a fair offer. I am open to discussing a range once we align on the role.
```

If HR insists:

```text
Based on my experience and the responsibilities discussed, I am expecting around <range>. However, I am open to discussing the complete compensation structure, including fixed pay, variable pay, benefits, joining bonus, and growth opportunity.
```

## Should You Give A Range?

Usually yes, if pushed.

Example:

```text
I am expecting in the range of <X> to <Y>, depending on fixed/variable structure, benefits, and role expectations.
```

Why range is useful:

- gives flexibility
- avoids locking too low
- allows HR to work internally
- sounds professional

Avoid:

```text
Anything is fine.
As per company norms.
I need maximum.
I will not join below exact number.
```

## Current CTC Question

Answer:

```text
My current CTC is <current CTC>, with <fixed> fixed and <variable> variable.
```

If you do not want to reveal too early:

```text
I can share the details during compensation discussion. At this stage, I would prefer to first understand the role fit and expectations.
```

But in India hiring process, HR often asks current CTC directly. Be factual.

## Expected CTC Question

Answer:

```text
Considering my current compensation, 6.5+ years of frontend experience, Angular expertise, and the responsibilities of this role, I am expecting around <range>.

I am flexible to discuss based on the complete offer structure and growth opportunity.
```

## If HR Says Budget Is Lower

Do not reject immediately.

Say:

```text
I understand. Can you please share the complete compensation structure, including fixed component, variable, joining bonus, benefits, and appraisal cycle?

If the fixed component and role growth are strong, I am open to evaluating it.
```

If still too low:

```text
I appreciate the offer, but based on my experience and current market range, this is lower than what I am targeting. Is there any flexibility in the fixed component or joining bonus?
```

## If HR Asks Your Minimum

Do not reveal your lowest number too early.

Better:

```text
I am targeting around <expected range>. My decision will depend on the complete structure, role, project, and long-term growth.
```

If they force:

```text
For me to make a confident move, I would prefer the offer to be closer to <number/range>, especially in fixed compensation.
```

## If HR Says "We Can Give Only 20% Hike"

Answer:

```text
I understand the policy. At the same time, my expectation is based on my experience, current skill set, and the role responsibilities.

Is there any flexibility through fixed pay adjustment, joining bonus, retention bonus, or early appraisal cycle?
```

## If HR Asks "Do You Have Another Offer?"

If no:

```text
Currently I am in discussion with a few companies, but I do not have a finalized offer yet. I am focusing on the right role and long-term growth, not only compensation.
```

If yes:

```text
Yes, I have an offer/discussion in progress. However, I am still evaluating based on role, project, company, compensation structure, and long-term growth.
```

## If HR Asks "Will You Join If We Match Your Expected?"

Answer:

```text
Compensation is definitely important, but my final decision will also depend on role clarity, project fit, work culture, location/work mode, and growth opportunity.

If these are aligned and the offer is close to my expectation, I would be positive about joining.
```

## Fixed Vs Variable

Always check:

- fixed salary
- variable pay
- performance bonus
- joining bonus
- retention bonus
- insurance
- gratuity
- PF
- stock/ESOP if any
- relocation support
- appraisal cycle

Important:

```text
High CTC with low fixed can be weaker than slightly lower CTC with strong fixed.
```

## Joining Bonus

Ask when:

- fixed is lower than expected
- you lose bonus in current company
- joining requires relocation
- company cannot increase base

Script:

```text
If the fixed component cannot be increased, is it possible to consider a joining bonus to bridge the gap?
```

## Notice Period And Early Joining

If HR wants early joining:

```text
My official notice period is <notice period>. After offer confirmation, I can discuss internally and try for early release. I will also make sure handover is managed properly.
```

Do not promise unrealistic date before confirming.

## Multiple Offer Handling

If you have multiple offers:

```text
I am evaluating opportunities based on role quality, technical growth, compensation, work culture, and long-term stability.
```

Avoid:

```text
Company X is giving more, so you must give more.
```

Better:

```text
I have another offer closer to my expected range. I am very interested in this opportunity as well. Is there any possibility to revise the compensation to make the decision easier?
```

## Offer Evaluation Checklist

Do not decide only by CTC.

Check:

- role
- project
- tech stack
- fixed salary
- variable salary
- joining bonus
- notice period buyout
- work mode
- location
- learning opportunity
- manager/team quality
- appraisal cycle
- company stability
- onsite/client exposure if relevant
- long-term career value

## How To Ask For Time

```text
Thank you for the offer. I am excited about the opportunity. I would like to review the compensation structure and discuss with my family. Can I get 2-3 days to confirm?
```

## How To Negotiate After Offer

```text
Thank you for sharing the offer. I appreciate the opportunity and I am interested in the role.

After reviewing the compensation, I feel the offer is slightly below my expectation considering my 6.5+ years of Angular/frontend experience, performance/accessibility/testing exposure, and the responsibilities discussed.

Is there any possibility to revise the fixed component closer to <number/range>?
```

## If Offer Is Good

```text
Thank you for the offer. The role and compensation look aligned with my expectations. I am positive about moving ahead. Please share the next steps and required documents.
```

## If Offer Is Low But Role Is Good

```text
I am interested in the role and the project looks aligned with my experience.

The only concern is the compensation. Is there any flexibility in fixed pay, joining bonus, or early appraisal cycle so that we can make this work?
```

## If You Need To Decline Politely

```text
Thank you for the offer and for the time throughout the process.

After careful consideration, I have decided not to move ahead because the compensation/role alignment is not matching my current expectations.

I appreciate the opportunity and hope we can connect again in the future.
```

## Mistakes To Avoid

- saying yes immediately without reading offer
- focusing only on total CTC
- ignoring fixed vs variable
- sounding desperate
- bluffing fake offers
- being rude or emotional
- changing expected amount repeatedly without reason
- accepting verbally and then backing out casually
- not checking work mode/location
- not asking about appraisal cycle

## Strong Salary Negotiation Script

```text
Thank you for sharing the offer. I am genuinely interested in the role because it aligns well with my Angular/frontend experience and long-term growth direction.

After reviewing the compensation, I feel there is a small gap compared to my expectation and current market range for my experience.

Considering my 6.5+ years of experience, Angular and TypeScript depth, enterprise project exposure, performance/accessibility/testing work, and mentoring responsibilities, I was expecting something closer to <expected range>.

Is there any flexibility to improve the fixed component or add a joining bonus?
```

## Final Interview Salary Answer

```text
For compensation, I am looking for a fair offer based on my 6.5+ years of Angular/frontend experience, current market standards, and the responsibilities of this role.

My preference is a strong fixed component and a role where I can contribute technically and grow long term. I am open to discussing the complete structure.
```

## One-Line Principle

```text
Negotiate with confidence, but keep the relationship positive.
```
